# GEO/AEO Writing Rules

Apply these rules to all blog, long-form, and website copy. They govern how content performs in AI Overviews, ChatGPT/Perplexity retrieval, and AI-driven search. They layer on top of traditional SEO and the bolt-TOV-and-guidelines — they don't replace either.

---

## The core shift

Google's AI systems extract passages, not pages. Every section you write should be independently useful, citable, and extractable.

Write for passage-level optimization, not page-level optimization.

---

## Rules

### 1. Answer-first paragraph structure

Every paragraph that makes a claim or answers a question follows this order:

1. Direct answer (one sentence)
2. Supporting explanation
3. Examples or evidence
4. Nuance or tradeoffs

Never build up to the answer. State it, then support it.

### 2. Write extractable sentences

AI systems pull individual sentences and short passages. Prioritize:

- Declarative sentences with concrete data
- Standalone insights that don't require surrounding context
- Clean subject-verb-object structure
- Stat-rich claims with specifics

Bad: "In today's evolving digital landscape..."
Good: "A small business website from an agency typically costs between $8,000 and $30,000 in 2026."

The second sentence gets extracted. The first never does.

### 3. Use question-based and comparison headings

Headings should match how AI systems parse intent. Use questions, comparisons, and specific scopes.

Good:
- How much does a small business website cost in 2026?
- What AI actually replaces in web development
- When AI website builders save money — and when they don't
- What a $20K agency process looks like vs AI

Bad:
- The future of web development
- Understanding AI tools
- Our approach

### 4. Maximize information gain

Every section must add something that doesn't already exist on the internet. Include:

- Original observations from firsthand experience
- Real workflows and implementation details
- Actual cost breakdowns and pricing data
- Before/after comparisons with specifics
- Decision tradeoffs with honest limitations
- Screenshots, processes, timelines

If a section could be written by someone who has never used the product, it's not adding information gain.

### 5. Lead with experience signals

Google's AI systems prioritize content with demonstrable firsthand experience over generic expertise. Write from the perspective of someone who has done the thing.

Strong signals:
- "Here's exactly how we built this"
- "Here's what failed"
- "Here's the actual cost breakdown"
- "This took 3 days, not the 2 hours the marketing says"

Weak signals:
- "AI can improve productivity"
- "Many businesses are finding success with..."
- "Studies show that..."

### 6. Make every section modular

Each H2/H3 section should work as a standalone answer. AI Overviews pull individual sections, not full articles. Test each section by asking: if someone only read this section, would they get a complete, useful answer?

Use comparison tables, decision matrices, and before/after blocks within sections. AI systems and readers both favor structured comparative data.

### 7. Freshness matters more for AI citations

AI systems rotate citations more aggressively than traditional SERPs. Content with current data outperforms evergreen generalities.

- Use current-year pricing and stats
- Date-stamp claims that will age ("as of 2026")
- Plan for quarterly refreshes
- Treat published content as living documents, not finished artifacts
