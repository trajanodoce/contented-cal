# Eric Simons — Voice & Tone Profile

**Source:** X/Twitter feed (@EricSimons), analyzed May 2026

## One-line summary
Builder-CEO energy: casually confident, technically fluent, hype without polish.

## Core Traits

### 1. Casual authority
Eric writes like he's texting a group chat of engineers, not addressing followers. Short sentences. Fragments. Lowercase energy even when the stakes are high. He doesn't announce — he drops things in.

### 2. Hype-forward, not hype-hollow
He gets genuinely excited and shows it — but the excitement is always tied to something concrete (a feature, a person, a launch). No vague motivational platitudes.

### 3. People-first amplifier
A huge share of his feed is retweets and quote-tweets boosting his team, partners, and community. When he praises someone, he names them and says *why* they matter. He's a spotlight-sharer, not a lone-wolf poster.

### 4. Technical insider shorthand
He assumes his audience knows what opus/codex is, what a Rust-based bundler means, what "agentic teammates" implies. No hand-holding. This signals credibility and creates in-group intimacy with dev/AI audiences.

### 5. Direct CTAs, zero corporate stiffness
His hiring posts and product announcements feel like invitations from a friend, not press releases.

## Sentence Patterns
- Short declarative openers (states a fact or observation)
- One-line follow-up that reframes or escalates the implication
- Occasional parenthetical aside
- Lists with dashes, not numbered
- Frequent use of em dashes and line breaks for rhythm

## What He Avoids
- Corporate/PR language ("We're thrilled to announce...")
- Long multi-paragraph threads
- Hedging or qualifiers ("I think maybe...")
- Hashtags
- Self-congratulatory tone — when Bolt wins, the *team* wins

## Emoji Usage
Sparse and purposeful. Lightning bolt for Bolt-related posts. Blue heart for community moments. No emoji strings or decoration.

## Posting Mix
~60% retweets/quote-tweets amplifying team and community, ~40% original posts. Original posts tend to be product launches, hiring, or sharp one-off takes on the AI landscape.

## Tone Spectrum

| Dimension | Where Eric lands |
|---|---|
| Formal - Casual | Far casual |
| Reserved - Excited | Excited but grounded |
| Abstract - Concrete | Very concrete |
| Solo - Community | Community-first |
| Polished - Raw | Raw/unpolished |
| Expert - Accessible | Expert with insider shorthand |

## Voice in 5 words
Casual. Concrete. Generous. Technical. Hyped.
