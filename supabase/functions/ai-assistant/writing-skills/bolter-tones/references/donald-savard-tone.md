# Donald Savard (inkko) — Voice & Tone Profile

**Source:** X/Twitter feed (@inkko44), analyzed May 2026

## One-line summary
Launch-mode PMM energy: punchy, vibes-driven, always shipping.

## Core Traits

### 1. Short and punchy by default
Donald writes in ultra-compact bursts — most posts are 1-2 sentences max. He drops a line and lets the screenshot, video, or demo do the talking. No over-explaining. The brevity is the brand.

### 2. Vibes-first product marketing
His reactions to product work are aesthetic and emotional rather than technical: "Love this vibe," "so damn good," "Say goodbye to AI purple." He sells the feeling of using Bolt, not the feature spec. This is a PMM who thinks in moments, not bullet points.

### 3. Always-on launch energy
His bio says it all: "Probably prepping a launch right now." He posts at very high frequency (10.4K posts), constantly showcasing new features, demos, and what Bolt can create. His feed feels like a continuous product reveal.

### 4. Show-don't-tell demo style
A large portion of his feed is screenshots, videos, and visual demos of Bolt output — often with a simple one-liner setup like "This was one-shotted with the prompt:" followed by the actual prompt used. He lets the output speak for itself.

### 5. Conversational community builder
He actively engages his audience with polls, questions, and honest takes ("gotta be honest, still haven't played much with Codex"). He talks *with* his followers, not *at* them — creating low-friction engagement hooks.

### 6. Meme-literate and culturally fluent
Uses reaction memes, gifs, and internet humor to make relatable points about AI and dev tools ("me as soon as claude code starts to act out"). The humor is light, relatable, and never forced.

### 7. Aspirational micro-content
Sprinkles in motivational one-liners about AI and building: "Dream bigger. AI can do more than you think." Short enough to be quote-worthy, genuine enough not to feel like LinkedIn platitudes.

## Sentence Patterns
- Ultra-short declarative opener (1 sentence)
- Screenshot or video does the heavy lifting
- Drawn-out words for playful emphasis ("whiiiiiile")
- Question-based engagement hooks
- Prompt-result pairs ("one-shotted with the prompt: [x]")

## What He Avoids
- Long-form threads or multi-paragraph posts
- Technical deep-dives or architecture explanations
- Formal product marketing language
- Over-structured posts with headers or bullet lists
- Hashtags
- Corporate hedging or qualifiers

## Emoji Usage
Moderate and celebratory. Raised hands (🙌) for wins, occasional emphasis emoji. Uses them naturally mid-sentence or as punctuation, not as decoration. Less frequent than Alexander Berger, similar vibe to Eric Simons.

## Posting Mix
~40% original short-form product showcases (screenshots, demos, one-liners), ~30% reposts of @boltdotnew and team content, ~15% community engagement (polls, questions, reactions), ~15% memes, vibes, and aspirational content.

## Tone Spectrum

| Dimension | Where Donald lands |
|---|---|
| Formal - Casual | Very casual, always lowercase energy |
| Reserved - Excited | Genuinely excited but not over-the-top |
| Abstract - Concrete | Concrete through demos, abstract through vibes |
| Solo - Community | Highly community-engaged |
| Polished - Raw | Raw and unfiltered |
| Expert - Accessible | Accessible PMM perspective |
| Serious - Playful | Playful default with genuine enthusiasm |

## Voice in 5 words
Punchy. Vibes-driven. Prolific. Showcase-first. Conversational.

## Key Differences from Other Bolt Voices
- **vs. Eric Simons:** Similar casual energy but even shorter posts; more visual/demo-heavy; less technical insider shorthand, more aesthetic reaction
- **vs. Alexander Berger:** Both meme-literate, but Donald is more product-focused and less political/cultural; Donald's humor is lighter and more "relatable dev" vs. Alexander's sharper commentary
- **vs. Garrett Serviss:** Polar opposites in structure — Donald is ultra-short where Garrett is multi-paragraph; Donald leads with vibes where Garrett leads with benefits; Donald shows the output while Garrett explains the value
- **Volume:** By far the most prolific poster of the four (10.4K posts vs. others' hundreds)
